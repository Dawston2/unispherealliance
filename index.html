UNISPHERE ALLIANCE

Driving community health impact across Ghana through decades of innovative HIV, TB, malaria, and epidemic response initiatives in partnership with national and global health agencies.

About Us

Unisphere Alliance was originally incorporated as UNISPHERE in January 2004 and re-registered in January 2021. The organization's head office is located at House No. 40 Korley Kojo Street, Odorkor, in the Ablekuma North Municipality in the Greater Accra Region, with a regional office at G U 917 Gumani, Tamale, in the Northern Region.

Unisphere Alliance was founded by Mr. Ernest Amoabeng Ortsin who is currently the President Ghana HIV and AIDS Network (GHANET). Over the past 20 years the organization has undertaken several health related projects in partnership with institutions such as Ghana AIDS Commission (GAC), National AIDS Control Programme (NACP), UNAIDS, Hope for Future Generations (HFFG), Ghana HIV and AIDS Network (GHANET) and STOP TB Partnership Ghana (STBPG) and Christian Health Association of Ghana (CHAG).

Our Projects

Implementation of HIV Self-Test project in Ledzokuku-Krowor Municipality in the Greater Ablekuma North Municipality in the Greater Accra as part of Global Fund NFM III (2021 – 2023) and in Ablekuma North Municipality as part of Global Fund Grant Cycle Seven (2024 – 2026).

Implementation of TB Case Detection in Gomoa West District sponsored by STOP TB Partnership Ghana and National TB Control Programme (NTP) as part of Global Fund Grant Cycle Seven (2024 – 2026).

Implementation of Community-Level Behaviour Change Communication (BCC) on Malaria Elimination in Tolon district, in the Northern Region, sponsored by National Malaria Elimination Programme (NMEP) as part of Global Fund NFM III (2021 – 2023) and Global Fund Grant Cycle Seven (2024 – 2026).

Implementation of TB Case Detection in Gomoa West District sponsored by STOP TB Partnership Ghana and Christian Health Association of Ghana as part of Global Fund NFM III (2021 – 2023).

Implementation of Community Sensitization activities on Ebola in Jamestown (Accra) under the auspices of UNAIDS (2015).

Implementation of HIV and AIDS activities in communities in Asiedu Keteke Sub-Metro sponsored by Ghana HIV and AIDS Network (2010).

Implementation of HIV and AIDS activities under the Community Communication Enhancement (CCE) project in Okaikoi North Sub-Metro sponsored by Hope for Future Generations (2009).

Implementation of HIV and AIDS activities under the Multisectoral Strengthening of HIV and AIDS Response Programme (MSHARP) under the auspices of the Ghana AIDS Commission (2008).

Implementation of HIV and AIDS activities under the Ghana AIDS Response Fund (GARFUND) under the auspices of the Ghana AIDS Commission (2005).
